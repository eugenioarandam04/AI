About Us

Shared Mission

We are two future qualified lawyers who identified a need for a system that assists both legal professionals and the general public in better understanding their legal position in matters such as leases, credit arrangements, and default interest. We recognize that calculating and interpreting these elements can often be complex and challenging.

Purpose

In response, we developed this application as a practical tool to simplify these processes. The app is designed for educational purposes and aims to provide users with meaningful insights from both a legal and an economic perspective.

Law And Economics

From a law and economics standpoint, the app’s functions reflect the connection between legal rules and their financial consequences:

Lease Analysis: Legal provisions define the rights and obligations within the lease, including duration, payment terms, and conditions. Economics helps assess the overall financial burden, including rent, deposits, and additional costs over time.

Credit Analysis and Comparator: The law establishes the terms of borrowing, repayment obligations, and enforceability, while economics evaluates interest rates, total cost of credit, and financial sustainability. The comparator further allows users to assess which option is more efficient or suitable based on their circumstances.

Default Interest: Legal rules govern when default interest applies and how it is calculated. Economics explains how such interest accumulates over time and increases the total amount owed, highlighting the financial consequences of non-compliance.

Damages Estimator: The law determines the conditions under which damages may be claimed and the types of losses that may be recoverable. Economics assists in quantifying those losses by distinguishing between direct and indirect damages and estimating their potential impact.

Why It Matters

By combining these perspectives, the app enables users to understand not only the legal structure of their obligations but also their practical and financial implications.
